Understanding Currency Pairs in Forex Trading

Welcome to our tutorial on major, minor, and exotic currency pairs, how to read them, and an overview of popular currency pairs in the forex market. This guide will provide you with the necessary knowledge to navigate the intricacies of currency trading.

Step 1: Major, Minor, and Exotic Currency Pairs

Currency pairs are categorized into major, minor, and exotic. Major pairs involve the most traded currencies globally, minors include currencies from smaller economies, and exotics consist of one major currency and one from a developing or small economy.

Step 2: How to Read a Currency Pair

A currency pair is displayed in a format such as EUR/USD, where the first currency (EUR) is the base currency, and the second (USD) is the quote currency. The exchange rate indicates how much of the quote currency is needed to purchase one unit of the base currency.

Step 3: Overview of Popular Currency Pairs

Popular currency pairs, also known as major pairs, include EUR/USD, USD/JPY, and GBP/USD. These pairs are highly traded and have liquidity, making them suitable for beginners. Understanding the characteristics of each pair is crucial for successful trading.
